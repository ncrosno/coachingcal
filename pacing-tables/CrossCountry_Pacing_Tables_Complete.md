# Cross Country Training Pace Tables
*Based on current 1600m PR - Modern Exercise Science 2025*

## How to Use These Tables
1. **Find your current 1600m PR** in the left column of any table
2. **Use the corresponding pace** for each type of workout
3. **Print individual tables** for easy reference at practice
4. **Adjust for conditions** - add 10-20 seconds/mile for heat/humidity

## Training Zone Definitions
- **Zone 2**: Easy/recovery runs (conversational pace)
- **LT1**: Moderate tempo (aerobic threshold - can maintain 45-60 min)
- **LT2**: Hard tempo (lactate threshold - 20-40 min efforts)
- **5K Race Pace**: Current race pace for intervals and time trials
- **VO2max**: Short hard intervals (3-8 minutes)

## Important Notes
- **All paces are per mile unless noted**
- **Adjust for weather**: Add 10-20 sec/mile for heat/humidity  
- **Use RPE as backup**: If pace feels wrong, trust your body
- **HRV monitoring overrides**: If HRV is low, do Zone 2 regardless of plan
- **Heat adaptation**: First 2 weeks may require slower paces
- **Individual variation**: Some athletes may need ±5-10 sec/mile adjustments

## RPE (Rate of Perceived Effort) Guide
- **Zone 2**: 3-4/10 - Can sing, nasal breathing possible
- **LT1**: 5-6/10 - Conversational but breathing deeper  
- **LT2**: 7-8/10 - Can speak short phrases, controlled hard effort
- **5K Race**: 8-9/10 - Very hard but sustainable for 5K distance
- **VO2max**: 9-10/10 - Maximum sustainable effort for 3-8 minutes
